Real talk

This isn't a shortcut
built by someone selling shortcuts.

Every job tool you've seen works the same way: text box, generic output, no memory, $20/month. They exist because someone realized they could charge for a chatbot with a nicer font. The person who built it doesn't use it. They don't need to. They already have a job.

Four internships at a Fortune 500. I built J.O.B.Z.E. because the math stopped making sense.

To actually compete for a role right now, you need a tailored resume and a tailored cover letter, framed specifically for that job, that company, that description. Everyone knows this. The problem is you also need to be firing off applications constantly because the market doesn't wait. And doing both of those things (personalizing everything while applying at volume) takes more hours than anyone actually has.

So I built a workflow. At first it just found jobs for me. Then I added preference filters. Then I taught it how to read my resume and match against listings properly. Then I built the skills: resume rewrites, cover letters, interview prep, company research. Each one tuned to do what I was spending hours doing manually.

What started as one automation to save myself time turned into a full system with hard rules, persistent memory, and six specialized skills that handle every part of the job search that used to eat my entire week.

I use it myself. Every week. This isn't a demo product. It's the tool I needed and couldn't find anywhere else.

The beta exists because I want to hear what's broken in your job search and build that into the system too. Not to hit a revenue number. To actually solve the problem.

If you want a shortcut, there are cheaper ones. If you want something that works, you're in the right place.
